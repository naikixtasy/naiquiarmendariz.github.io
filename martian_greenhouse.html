Martian Greenhouse Project

Exploring Sustainable Life on Mars

About the Project

The Martian Greenhouse Project is a NASA-led STEM initiative that brings together student teams from across the country to design sustainable food production systems for space habitats. As part of this effort, I contributed to the development of a compact, modular greenhouse designed to operate in Martian-like environments using a soil-free, aeroponic plant system.

My Role & Technical Contributions

I worked as a lead designer and documentation specialist for our team's plant chamber prototype. My tasks included:

  • Designing a 3D-printed removable rack system for vertical aeroponic farming
  • Integrating a water-efficient, soil-free misting system controlled via microcontroller
  • Modeling LED lighting that simulates Martian sunlight conditions
  • Documenting technical specifications, diagrams, and project milestones
  • Collaborating with NASA engineers and STEM mentors in Zoom workshops

Why It Matters

This project simulates real-world constraints astronauts will face when growing food beyond Earth. From lighting and water efficiency to portability and self-sufficiency, every design decision plays a role in supporting future long-term missions on Mars or the Moon.
