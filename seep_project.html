About the Project

Our student research team from El Paso Community College was selected by the Student Spaceflight Experiments Program (SSEP) to design and test an experiment for flight aboard the International Space Station. Our investigation focused on the ability of Opuntia ficus-indica (cactus) mucilage to remove chromium from contaminated water under microgravity conditions. The project was presented during the National SSEP Showcase at the Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum.

Project Goal

We aimed to evaluate whether the natural mucilage in cactus could effectively bind and remove harmful heavy metals from water in space-based environments. The outcome of this research has implications for long-term space travel and future off-Earth habitats.

My Role

As Co-Principal Investigator, I collaborated closely with faculty mentors, lab technicians, and chemistry professors. I contributed to experiment planning, lab protocol execution, and results documentation. The project was a significant step in my research career and strengthened my passion for aerospace and bioresource engineering.
