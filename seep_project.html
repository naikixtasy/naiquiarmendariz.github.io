SEEP – NASA Student Spaceflight Experiment

"The Effect of Microgravity on the Use of Cactus Mucilage for Water Purification"

About the Program

The Student Spaceflight Experiments Program (SSEP), hosted by the National Center for Earth and Space Science Education, provides students with the opportunity to design and propose real microgravity experiments to be conducted aboard the International Space Station (ISS).

Project Overview

Our experiment was selected for flight from El Paso, Texas as part of Mission 1 to ISS. We tested whether Opuntia ficus-indica (prickly pear cactus) mucilage could purify chromium-contaminated water under microgravity. This research supports long-term space exploration goals by investigating natural water purification methods in space.

Role and Contribution

As a Co-Principal Investigator, I participated in the proposal development, experimental design, and presentation of findings. I collaborated closely with our local college team and guided the formulation of a testable hypothesis grounded in space science. Our findings contribute to future approaches for sustainable water systems on the Moon or Mars.
